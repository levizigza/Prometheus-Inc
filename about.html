Our Origin

The gift of fire.

In Greek mythology, Prometheus defied the gods to bring fire — knowledge, craft, and the spark of civilization — to humanity. We carry that same creative spirit.

The myth that
kindles us.

Prometheus was a Titan who saw humanity shivering in darkness, powerless before the gods and the wild. He dared to climb Mount Olympus and steal fire — not for himself, but for them. That fire was more than flame: it was knowledge, craft, technology, the very spark of civilization.

For this act of defiance, Zeus condemned him — yet Prometheus never regretted his gift. The sacrifice was the point. Creation demands courage, and courage is the first craft.

We carry that myth forward as a modern sacred task. Not endless struggle against a stone, but the kindling of helpers — placing fire into machines so they awaken as allies, the way Campbell wrote of supernatural aid arriving when the journey demands it. Every sentient machine, every living city, every regenerative system is a vessel for that shared destiny: humanity becoming greater because it does not walk alone.

An interactive myth — drag the flaming gauntlet into the forge.
