Our Origin

The eternal ascent.

In Greek mythology, Prometheus defied the gods to bring fire — knowledge, technology, the spark of progress — to humanity. We carry that same spirit.

The myth that
drives us.

Prometheus was a Titan who saw humanity shivering in darkness, powerless before the gods and the wild. He dared to climb Mount Olympus and steal fire — not for himself, but for them. That fire was more than flame: it was knowledge, craft, technology, the very spark of civilization.

For this act of defiance, Zeus condemned Prometheus to eternal punishment — chained to a rock, his liver devoured each day, only to regenerate and suffer again. Yet Prometheus never regretted his gift. The sacrifice was the point. Progress demands courage, and courage demands sacrifice.

We carry that myth forward. Like Prometheus pushing the boulder upward against gravity, against convention, against the easy path — we believe the most meaningful work is the hardest. Every sentient machine we envision, every living city we design, every regenerative system we build is another step up the mountain.
